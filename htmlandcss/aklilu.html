Doctor Aklilu Lemma






Aklilu Lemma (Amharic: አክሊሉ ለማ; September 18, 1935 – April 5, 1997) was an Ethiopian pathologist. He made significant contributions to the study of tropical diseases, particularly schistosomiasis, and to the fight against malaria in Ethiopia.
He was also a pioneer in the study of the medicinal properties of plants native to Ethiopia.

Early Life and Education

  • Aklilu Lemma was born in Jijiga, in what was then the Hararghe Province of the Ethiopian Empire (now part of
    the Somali Region, Ethiopia).

  • He pursued his education at Addis Ababa University College and later earned his D.Sc. from Johns Hopkins University
    in the United States in 1964. His doctoral research focused on sandfly-borne leishmaniasis.

    • Career Highlights

  • After completing his studies, Lemma returned to Ethiopia and began working at the university as a professor of
       parasitology. He also served as the director of the university's Institute of Pathobiology.
  • He was a founding member of the Ethiopian Academy of Sciences and served as its president from 1980 to 1986.
  • He was also a member of the World Health Organization's Expert Advisory Panel on Parasitic Diseases.
  • Lemma was a pioneer in the study of the medicinal properties of plants native to Ethiopia. He discovered the
       anti-bilharzia properties of the plant Warburgia salutaris, which led to the development of the drug praziquantel.
  • He also discovered the anti-malarial properties of the plant Artemisia annua, which led to the development of the drug artemisinin.
  • Lemma was a vocal advocate for the use of traditional medicine in Ethiopia and worked to promote the use of
    traditional medicinal plants in the treatment of diseases.
  • He was also a strong advocate for the conservation of Ethiopia's natural resources and worked to promote the
    sustainable use of the country's biodiversity.
  • Lemma was the recipient of numerous awards and honors for his work, including the Right Livelihood Award in 1989.
  • He was also awarded the Order of the Star of Ethiopia by Emperor Haile Selassie in 1966.
  • Lemma passed away on April 5, 1997, at the age of 61.
